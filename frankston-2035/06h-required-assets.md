# Required Assets
## First Nations Protocol — Frankston 2035

*Everything Blue Snake Studio needs to have, make, find, or commission before this protocol can be used operationally.*

---

## Critical Path — Nothing Moves Without These

These assets block everything. No First Nations engagement can occur until they exist.

---

### Asset 1 — Independent Cultural Safety Advisor (ENGAGEMENT)

**What it is:** A qualified Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander professional engaged to review this protocol before it is used.

**What they must be:**
- Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander identity
- Professional experience in cultural safety in arts, community, or institutional contexts
- Not affiliated with BSS, Council, or any project partner
- Working in a professional capacity (not as a favour)

**What they are asked to do:**
- Review the full 9-part protocol document (06)
- Provide written feedback identifying gaps, errors, or risks
- Indicate whether the document is ready to use or requires amendment
- Note any specific concerns for the Frankston/Bunurong context

**How to find them:**
- Creative Victoria's First Nations arts contacts
- Regional Arts Victoria First Nations programs
- Aboriginal Victoria (State Government)
- RMIT or Deakin Indigenous Studies programs (for referrals, not engagement)
- Do not approach individuals cold — seek a referral from a trusted institutional intermediary

**Timeline:** Before any operational use of the protocol. Non-negotiable.

**Fee:** Professional rate — budget $1,500–$3,000 for review and written report.

---

### Asset 2 — Written Permission Document Template — Legal Review

**What it is:** The permission document described in Part 5 of the protocol, reviewed by a lawyer with experience in First Nations IP and cultural rights.

**Why it matters:** The template in Appendix D of the Playbook is a starting point, not a legal document. Before it is presented to any community member or organisation, it must be reviewed to confirm it is appropriate, enforceable, and not inadvertently harmful.

**What the review covers:**
- Legal accuracy under Australian IP law and cultural rights frameworks
- Appropriate framing for a non-commercial civic arts context
- Language that is clear to a non-legal reader
- Absence of clauses that could be read as a waiver of rights

**How to find a reviewer:** Arts Law Centre of Australia (artslaw.com.au) — offers legal advice to arts organisations including specific guidance on First Nations IP.

**Fee:** Budget $500–$1,500 for legal review.

## Required Before First Contact

These assets are needed before BSS initiates any First Nations engagement.

---

### Asset 3 — Project Research Summary

**What it is:** A written summary of publicly available information about:
- The Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation — who they are, their formal processes, their contact process
- Any registered cultural heritage sites in Frankston and the immediate project areas
- Any existing CHMPs or Aboriginal Heritage Assessment Reports for relevant sites (available via Aboriginal Victoria Heritage Register)
- Arts organisations and community services with established First Nations relationships in the Frankston area

**Who produces it:** Creative Director (with possible assistance from a researcher)
**How long it takes:** 2–3 weeks of focused research
**Format:** A written summary document, saved to project records
**Cost:** Internal time only (unless a research assistant is engaged)

---

### Asset 4 — Intermediary Mapping

**What it is:** A short list of organisations or individuals who may be able to make a respectful introduction to the Bunurong community or First Nations services in Frankston.

**Potential intermediary types:**
- Frankston City Council's cultural heritage team (if they have existing relationships)
- Peninsula Health Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Team
- Local Aboriginal Education Consultative Groups (LAECGs) connected to Frankston schools
- Koorie Heritage Trust (Melbourne-based but may advise)
- Regional arts networks with First Nations partnerships

**What an intermediary does:** Makes an introduction only. Does not speak on BSS's behalf. Does not advocate for the project. Does not negotiate.

**What an intermediary does not do:** Provide cultural permission. Substitute for direct relationship. Speak for any community.

**Format:** A one-page list with contact details and the nature of each relationship — internal document only.

---

### Asset 5 — Internal Protocol Briefing (Team Training)

**What it is:** A 60–90 minute internal briefing session for all BSS personnel who will work on Frankston 2035.

**What it covers:**
- The key commitments of the protocol
- What each team member must and must not do
- How to handle a situation not covered by the protocol
- How to log decisions and concerns

**Who delivers it:** Creative Director, using the bullet point version (06d) and memetic version (06f) as reference materials

**When:** Before any First Nations engagement begins. Refreshed annually.

**Cost:** Internal time only.

## Ongoing Operational Assets

These are needed as the project progresses.

---

### Asset 8 — Decision Log (Running Document)

**What it is:** A simple spreadsheet or document that records every decision made under the protocol.

**Columns:**
- Date
- Context (what project or situation)
- Decision made
- Who was involved
- What was documented
- Notes

**Who maintains it:** Creative Director.
**Where it lives:** Project records (git repository or secure shared folder).
**Cost:** None.

---

### Asset 9 — Annual Review Template

**What it is:** A structured review document, completed annually, assessing compliance with the protocol over the preceding twelve months.

**Sections:**
- Summary of First Nations engagements during the year
- Decisions made under the protocol
- Breaches (if any) and how they were handled
- Feedback received (with consent)
- Recommended amendments to the protocol
- Sign-off: Creative Director + external reviewer (if engaged)

**First review:** June 2027.
**Cost:** Internal time only (plus external reviewer if engaged — budget $500–$1,000).
