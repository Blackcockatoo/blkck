# First Nations Protocol — Council Reference Version
## Frankston 2035

*Prepared for: Frankston City Council officers and elected members*
*Purpose: To describe how Blue Snake Studio manages its obligations regarding First Nations cultural heritage and community engagement in this project.*

---

## Overview

Frankston 2035 is a public art and civic creativity initiative proposing to operate across public spaces, schools, and Frankston Library — all of which sit within the Country of the Bunurong people of the Kulin Nation, the Registered Aboriginal Party (RAP) for this area.

Blue Snake Studio has prepared a formal First Nations Protocol to govern how the organisation will approach this landscape responsibly. This document summarises that protocol for Council partners.

---

## The Legal and Cultural Context

**Aboriginal Heritage Act 2006 (Vic)** requires that certain activities on or near cultural heritage consult with the Registered Aboriginal Party. The Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation is the RAP for the Frankston area.

**Frankston 2035 projects** include physical installations in public space, school grounds, and library premises. Some of these may require Cultural Heritage Management Plans (CHMPs) or engagement with the RAP process depending on site and scope.

Blue Snake Studio's protocol governs internal process. Separately and in parallel, any statutory RAP obligations will be identified and fulfilled per the Act.

---

## What Blue Snake Studio Commits To

**1. Honest starting position**
BSS has no existing relationship with any First Nations community in Frankston. This is documented and acknowledged. The project does not claim community support that has not been established.

**2. Process before content**
No First Nations cultural material — language, symbols, stories, imagery — will be used in any project output without explicit, written, documented permission from the relevant community. This is non-negotiable and is not subject to creative or funding pressure.

**3. The correct sequence**
BSS follows a documented sequence for engagement: research, intermediary introduction, first listening contact, relationship development, then — and only then — any request for collaboration or permission. This sequence protects both the community and the project.

**4. Written permission for all cultural use**
All permissions are documented in writing, describing specifically what is permitted, for what purpose, by whom, and on what terms. Verbal understandings are not sufficient for any publicly facing element.

**5. Payment for expertise**
BSS pays for cultural advice and participation at professional rates. Unpaid "community contribution" of cultural expertise is not a practice of this project.

**6. Cultural safety audit**
Before any publicly visible artwork is finalised, an independent cultural safety review is commissioned from a qualified Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander professional. The report is kept permanently.

**7. No means no**
If a community, organisation, or individual declines to participate or grant permission, BSS accepts that decision, documents it, removes the relevant element from scope, and does not seek permission from a different source.

**8. Transparent breach reporting**
If BSS fails to follow its protocol, it reports that failure transparently to all project partners including Council. Breaches are not managed quietly.

**9. RAP compliance**
BSS will engage with the Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation through their formal process for any project element that triggers statutory requirements under the Aboriginal Heritage Act 2006 (Vic). This engagement is in addition to, not instead of, the relational process described in the full protocol.

## What This Means for Council's Risk Profile

| Risk | BSS Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Project uses First Nations cultural material without permission | Written permission required for all use. Cultural safety audit required before any public artwork is finalised. |
| Project misrepresents First Nations culture | Cultural safety advisor review before finalisation. 48-hour response protocol for any concern raised. |
| Project creates liability under Aboriginal Heritage Act | Statutory RAP engagement process followed in parallel with BSS's relational protocol. |
| Project claims community support that does not exist | BSS explicitly documents its starting position. No false claims in grant applications or public materials. |
| Community relationship deteriorates during project | Annual review. Transparent breach reporting. Protocol governs even when it is inconvenient. |

---

## What Council Is Asked To Do

**Active:**
- Facilitate introduction to Council's cultural heritage officers, who may have existing relationships or knowledge relevant to the Bunurong Land Council
- Advise BSS of any existing heritage assessments or cultural heritage management plans for proposed project sites

**Passive:**
- Do not require BSS to claim First Nations community support that has not been established
- Do not require BSS to move faster on First Nations engagement than the protocol allows
- Accept that some project elements may be removed from scope if permission is not granted, and support BSS in that outcome

---

## Current Status

This protocol is an internal working document. It has not yet been reviewed by any First Nations community or advisor.

**That review must occur before this protocol is cited publicly or before BSS initiates any First Nations engagement.**

Council is asked to note this status and to support BSS in securing a qualified cultural safety advisor for that review.
