# Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
## Project Constitution — Version 1.0

---

### What This Project Is

A ten-year civic creativity initiative centred in Frankston, Victoria.

Art becomes infrastructure.
Libraries become studios.
Murals become classrooms.
Technology serves children, not corporations.
The community is the client.

---

### North Star

By 2035, Frankston is a city where creative life is woven into daily life — in schools, libraries, streets, and screens — recognised locally and studied nationally as a model for civic creativity.

---

### Core Principles

1. **Frankston is the hero.** Every decision strengthens the city, not the agency.
2. **Art is infrastructure.** Public creativity carries the same weight as roads and parks.
3. **Children first.** No surveillance, no advertising, no dark patterns near young people.
4. **First Nations protocols are non-negotiable.** We listen before we act. We do not proceed without permission.
5. **Technology must earn its place.** Only tools that genuinely serve are permitted into programs.
6. **Replicability by design.** Everything built here can be adopted elsewhere.
7. **Blue Snake Studio is the engine, not the ego.** We serve the project, not the portfolio.
8. **Transparency protects the project.** Open records, clear governance, honest reporting.

---

### What This Project Is Not

- A branding exercise for any organisation
- A political campaign or advocacy platform
- A tourism or economic development strategy
- A technology startup
- A cultural content provider or publisher
- A claim over First Nations cultural material

---

### What Success Looks Like in 2035

- 50+ permanent public artworks across Frankston
- Every primary school with at least one living, curriculum-linked mural project
- Frankston Library recognised nationally as a creative infrastructure model
- The Frankston model replicated by at least three other Australian councils
- Zero surveillance technology in any BSS child-facing program
- First Nations relationships built on genuine, long-term, earned trust — not transaction
