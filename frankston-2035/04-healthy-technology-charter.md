# Healthy Technology Charter
## Frankston 2035

---

### Purpose

This Charter defines which technologies may and may not be used in Blue Snake Studio programs involving children and young people.

It exists because good intentions are not enough. Clear rules protect children, protect the project, and protect partners.

---

### The Three Questions

Before any technology enters a BSS program, it must answer yes to all three:

1. **Does it serve the child, not the corporation?**
   The technology's primary purpose must be the child's learning, creativity, or wellbeing — not data collection, engagement metrics, or commercial return.

2. **Does it work without an account?**
   Children in BSS programs do not create accounts, share email addresses, or submit personal information.

3. **Does it work offline, or can it?**
   Technology that requires continuous connectivity creates dependency and data exposure. Preference is given to tools that function fully offline.

---

### Permitted

| Category | Standard |
|---|---|
| Open-source creative tools | Freely permitted if they meet the three questions |
| Offline educational software | Freely permitted |
| Static websites (no tracking) | Freely permitted |
| Physical creative tools | Always permitted |
| QR-linked content (privacy-respecting) | Permitted with documentation |
| Audio recording (local only) | Permitted with participant consent |

---

### Not Permitted

| Category | Reason |
|---|---|
| Social media platforms | Designed for engagement, not learning |
| Apps requiring account creation | Creates data exposure for minors |
| Platforms with behavioural advertising | Exploits attention of children |
| Gamified reward systems (external) | Designed to create dependency |
| Surveillance or facial recognition | No context justifies this in a creative program |
| Cloud platforms without privacy audit | Default-off until audit is completed |

---

### The Grey Zone

Some tools fall between. When uncertain, the Creative Director makes the call, and the decision is documented in the Asset Register with reasoning.

No partner organisation can override this Charter without a formal Constitution-level amendment.

---

### For Partners

Schools, libraries, and Council agencies partnering on BSS programs are asked to:

- Review this Charter before program commencement
- Confirm which technology will be present in shared spaces
- Nominate a technology contact person
- Flag any technology conflicts in advance

This is not a compliance exercise. It is a shared commitment to children's safety.

---

### Review

This Charter is reviewed annually, or when a material technology change is proposed.
