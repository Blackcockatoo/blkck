# Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
## Vision One-Pager

*Print on A4. Hand to anyone. Keep it.*

---

## The Idea

Frankston has creative people. What it lacks is creative infrastructure — the systems that connect individual acts of creativity to civic life.

Frankston 2035 builds that infrastructure. Permanently. Starting now.

---

## Three Things That Will Exist

**A mural that teaches.**
Designed with students in a Frankston primary school. Curriculum-linked. On the wall for decades. Not decoration — a teaching tool with a resource pack that any teacher can use.

**A library where you make things.**
Frankston Library gets a dedicated creative technology space. Drawing. Music. Writing. Animation. No accounts. No tracking. No advertising. Just tools that serve the child.

**A walk that tells Frankston's story.**
Ten to fifteen stops through the CBD and foreshore. QR codes linking to local art, local voices, local history. No app. No download. Phone optional, wonder required.

---

## What Makes This Different

Every other creative program in this city produces an event. Frankston 2035 produces infrastructure.

When the funding ends, the mural is still on the wall.
When the program finishes, the library space is still open.
When the artist moves on, the walk is still there.

This is the difference between a project and a system.

2026–2027    Three pilots. Three permanent outcomes. One model proven.

2031–2035    Frankston becomes the model. Other councils adopt it.
             50+ permanent artworks. City known for civic creativity.

## Who Does This

**Blue Snake Studio** — Frankston-rooted creative practice. Public art, community programs, educational resources. The engine of this project, not the ego.

**Frankston City Council** — The civic partner. Opens doors. Co-invests. Points to the result. Frankston is always the hero.

**Local schools, libraries, and artists** — Own the outcomes. Run the programs. The work transfers to them on completion.
