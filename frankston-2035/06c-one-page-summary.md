# First Nations Protocol — One-Page Summary
## Frankston 2035 — Chunk 06

*The first step is not content creation. The first step is respectful process.*

*For the full 9-part treatment, see document 06. This summary does not replace it.*
*This document is internal. Not for external distribution until reviewed by a cultural safety advisor.*

---

**Part 1 — Preamble**
Frankston 2035 operates on Bunurong Country. The Bunurong people of the Kulin Nation are the Traditional Custodians of this land and its waters. Their sovereignty has never been ceded. Good intentions without good process cause harm. Blue Snake Studio starts from outside any existing First Nations relationship and says so plainly.

**Part 2 — What We Do Not Claim**
We do not claim to represent, speak for, or act on behalf of any First Nations community. We do not claim permission to use First Nations cultural material. We do not claim relationships that do not exist. We do not assume that one person speaks for their community. We do not treat Acknowledgement of Country as engagement.

**Part 3 — Relationship Before Project**
The sequence is fixed: Research → Intermediary → First Contact → Listening → Time → Relationship → (only then) Project. This sequence cannot be reversed, compressed, or faked. If a funding deadline requires faster engagement than genuine relationship allows, we decline the condition or the funding.

**Part 4 — The Listening Posture**
When in conversation with First Nations individuals or organisations, we listen. We do not record without consent. We do not share without permission. We do not fill silence. We follow up on commitments. We distinguish between listening and research — research extracts, listening receives.

**Part 5 — Seeking Permission**
Permission is specific, not general. Each use requires its own permission. Each permission is documented in writing. We describe exactly what is being asked, how it will be used, who will see it, and for how long. We allow weeks, not hours. We pay for cultural expertise at professional rates.

**Part 6 — When Permission Is Not Granted**
No means no. We do not reframe the ask. We do not ask a different person. We document the refusal and remove the element from scope. If permission is withdrawn after being granted, we stop immediately and bear the cost. No creative direction, no funding condition, no public commitment overrides this.

**Part 7 — Cultural Safety in Public Spaces**
Before any publicly visible artwork is finalised, an independent cultural safety advisor is commissioned to review it. The review is documented and kept permanently. Concerns raised about completed artworks are responded to within 48 hours. Cultural safety is designed in — not added at the end.

**Part 8 — When Projects Touch Country, Story, or Site**
Before any installation, the Creative Director researches cultural significance through public records, consults any existing advisors, and treats uncertainty as significance. The Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation is the Registered Aboriginal Party (RAP) for Frankston. RAP engagement follows its formal process under the Aboriginal Heritage Act 2006 (Vic). This protocol precedes but does not replace that process.

**Part 9 — Accountability and Review**
The Creative Director is personally responsible for this protocol. Every decision is logged. Breaches are reported transparently — not managed quietly. An independent cultural safety advisor reviews this document before operational use. The protocol is reviewed annually. The only measure of quality that matters: do First Nations people in Frankston, over time, describe Blue Snake Studio as an organisation that kept its word?
