# First Nations Protocol — Bullet Point Version
## Frankston 2035 — Chunk 06

*Quick reference. Full reasoning in document 06.*
*Internal only. Not for external distribution until reviewed.*

---

## Part 1 — Preamble

- Frankston 2035 operates on Bunurong Country (Kulin Nation). Sovereignty never ceded.
- Cultural significance exists in this landscape whether or not it is visible to us.
- Good intentions without good process cause harm. History shows this.
- BSS has no existing relationship with any First Nations community here. We say so openly.
- This protocol is not a checklist. It is a set of commitments held by internal log and external review.
- The first step is not content creation. The first step is respectful process.

---

## Part 2 — What We Do Not Claim

- We do not represent, speak for, or act on behalf of any First Nations community.
- We do not claim permission to use First Nations language, stories, symbols, or cultural material.
- We do not describe non-existent relationships as if they exist.
- We do not treat one community member's view as the community's view.
- We do not treat Acknowledgement of Country as meaningful engagement.
- We do not treat this protocol as sufficient without genuine relationship.
- We do not allow urgency to compress process.
- If a funder asks whether we have community support: we say "we are building towards it."

---

## Part 3 — Relationship Before Project

- The sequence: Research → Intermediary → First Contact → Listening → Time → Relationship → Project
- This sequence is not reversible. No step is skippable.
- First contact: a request for conversation. No brief. No budget. No timeline.
- Intermediaries where possible — arts organisations, community services, cultural networks.
- First conversation: we listen only. We do not pitch.
- If the relationship is not established when a pilot date approaches: the date moves.
- If a funder requires faster engagement than relationship allows: we decline the condition or the funding.

---

## Part 4 — The Listening Posture

- No recording without explicit informed consent.
- No note-sharing with third parties (Council, funders, partners) without explicit permission.
- No pre-formed creative direction brought to a listening session.
- No filling of silence — silence is part of the conversation.
- No commitments made in the moment.
- Notes written within 24 hours; shared back for accuracy check.
- Follow through on every commitment made.
- Listening ≠ research. Research extracts. Listening receives.
- Test: could I say something back that would make this person feel understood? If no — I was not listening.

---

## Part 5 — Seeking Permission

- Permission is specific, not general.
- Each of the following is a separate permission:
  - Conversation ≠ use of cultural material
  - One project ≠ next project
  - One person ≠ their community
  - This use ≠ another use of the same material
  - Verbal ≠ written (for anything publicly used)
- Written permission must describe: what, how, audience, duration, credit, ownership, conditions.
- Allow weeks, not hours. Say: "There is no hurry. Take the time you need."
- Receive the answer without negotiating it.
- If scope changes: seek fresh permission.
- Cultural expertise is paid at professional rates. ($150–250/hr minimum)
- Asking for free advice is exploitation regardless of the cause.

---

## Part 6 — When Permission Is Not Granted

- No means no. Not "try a different framing." Not "find a different person."
- On receiving a refusal: thank, do not probe for reasons, do not express disappointment as pressure.
- Document the refusal immediately.
- Remove the element from project scope immediately.
- Do not resurface it later unless the community raises it first.
- Do not share who declined or why — with anyone.
- If permission is withdrawn after being given: stop immediately; modify/remove; bear the cost.
- No deadline, funder condition, or creative preference overrides a withdrawal.

---

## Part 7 — Cultural Safety in Public Spaces

- Before any publicly visible artwork is finalised: commission an independent cultural safety review.
- The reviewer must be an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander professional with arts/cultural safety expertise.
- The reviewer is not affiliated with BSS or any project partner.
- The reviewer provides written findings.
- The audit covers: site, visual content, narrative, consultation process, permission documentation.
- The audit report is kept permanently.
- If a concern is raised about a completed work — at any time — respond within 48 hours and initiate a review.
- Cultural safety is not cultural avoidance. Frankston is on Country. That presence belongs in the project, carefully.

---

## Part 8 — When Projects Touch Country, Story, or Site

- Before any installation: research cultural significance (heritage registers, RAP maps, public records).
- Consult any existing advisors before proceeding.
- If significance is identified: do not proceed without community permission.
- If significance is uncertain: treat it as significant until clarified.
- Silence in public records ≠ absence of significance.
- The Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation is the RAP for Frankston under the Aboriginal Heritage Act 2006 (Vic).
- Engage the RAP through its formal process — not informally, not through intermediaries.
- This protocol does not replace the RAP process. It precedes it.
- Pilot-specific obligations:
  - Pilot 01 (Mural): site check and cultural safety audit before concept finalised.
  - Pilot 02 (Library): Acknowledgement in all materials; no First Nations content without permission.
  - Pilot 03 (QR Walk): every stop site-checked; Acknowledgement at first stop and on printed map.

---

## Part 9 — Accountability and Review

- The Creative Director is personally responsible for this protocol. Not delegable.
- Every decision under this protocol is logged: date, context, decision, who was consulted.
- Breaches (any bypass of this protocol) are reported transparently to all partners. Not managed quietly.
- A breach is: using material without permission, claiming non-existent relationships, bypassing the sequence, ignoring a refusal, sharing confidential information, not paying for expertise, overriding the protocol for any reason.
- Phase 1: Independent cultural safety advisor reviews this document before any engagement begins.
- Phase 2: Annual review against what happened, what worked, what didn't.
- Phase 3: End-of-pilot feedback sought from any First Nations individuals/organisations with prior contact.
- The only measurement that matters: do First Nations people in Frankston describe BSS as an organisation that kept its word?
