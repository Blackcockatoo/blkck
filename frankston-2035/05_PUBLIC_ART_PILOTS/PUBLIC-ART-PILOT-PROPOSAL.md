# Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
## Public Art Pilot Proposal

*For: Artists, arts administrators, funders, and public art committees*
*Prepared by: Blue Snake Studio*

---

## The Proposal

Three public art pilots. Three permanent outcomes. One city transformed.

Frankston 2035 is not a public art program. It is a civic creativity system that uses public art as one of its primary instruments. The difference is that everything produced is permanent, curriculum-linked, community-owned, and designed to grow.

---

## Pilot 01 — The Living Mural

### The Brief

Commission a professional artist to create a large-scale mural at a Frankston primary school.

The mural is co-designed with students. It is curriculum-linked — the visual content is built around learning areas identified by the school's teachers. It is permanent. After installation, it belongs to the school.

### Artistic Methodology

**Co-design, not consultation.**
Students are not surveyed and ignored. Their contributions drive the content. At minimum, one student-originated element must appear in the final artwork unchanged. This is a non-negotiable condition of commission.

**Curriculum as content.**
The artist works with teachers before beginning the design. The mural carries information — mathematical patterns, historical references, local ecology, language — embedded in visual form. This is what makes the wall teach.

**Artist as educator.**
The commissioned artist spends at least two sessions in the school before beginning — not to teach art, but to listen. To learn what the school cares about. The design comes after.

**The artwork is complete without documentation — but documentation is a deliverable.**
The artist is required to produce: one artist statement, one process diary (minimum five entries), and one photograph documentation set. These become part of the school's permanent record.

### Artist Brief (Summary)

Artists commissioned for the Living Mural are:
- Australian-based (Frankston or greater Victoria preferred but not required)
- Experienced in large-format exterior or interior installation
- Comfortable with participatory and co-design processes
- Prepared to credit the school's students as co-designers in all public documentation

Artist fees are benchmarked against NAVA recommended rates. Materials, installation scaffolding, and all permits are covered separately.

### Budget

| Line Item | Range |
|---|---|
| Artist fee (concept to completion) | $25,000–$35,000 |
| Materials and consumables | $5,000–$8,000 |
| Community facilitation and co-design | $5,000–$10,000 |
| Documentation and curriculum pack | $5,000–$7,000 |
| Permits, insurance, scaffolding | $5,000–$5,000 |
| **Total** | **$45,000–$65,000** |

### Deliverables

- One installed mural (permanent, on school property)
- One curriculum resource pack (teacher edition + student activity sheets)
- One artist documentation set (statement, process diary, photographs)
- One replication guide (so the next school can follow the process)

## Pilot 03 — The QR Art Walk

### The Brief

Commission a collection of local artists and community members to create content for a self-guided creative walk through Frankston's CBD and foreshore.

Ten to fifteen QR-coded stops. Each stop opens a static web page — no app, no account, no tracking — featuring original artwork, audio, photography, or writing created by local contributors.

### Artistic Methodology

**Community-nominated sites.**
The stops are not chosen by BSS or by Council. They are nominated by the community through a structured process: What places in Frankston matter to you? What story should be told here?

**Multiple forms of content.**
Each stop can carry different content types:
- Original artwork (digital or photographed physical)
- Audio recordings (music, spoken word, oral history)
- Photography (with consent)
- Writing (short form — poetry, place essays, personal histories)

**No platform dependency.**
All content is hosted on static web pages. The files are small. The pages load on slow connections. If the hosting ever fails, the content can be moved to a new host in an afternoon. No content is locked in a proprietary platform.

**Community additions.**
After launch, new stops can be added by community members following the documented process. The system is designed to grow without BSS involvement.

### Budget

| Line Item | Range |
|---|---|
| Content creation (commissioning local artists) | $10,000–$15,000 |
| Web build (static pages, QR system) | $3,000–$5,000 |
| Print (map, QR plaques) | $2,000–$3,000 |
| Physical installation (signage, weather-proofing) | $2,000–$4,000 |
| Documentation and replication guide | $3,000–$5,000 |
| **Total** | **$20,000–$35,000** |

### Deliverables

- 10–15 installed QR stops with weather-proof signage
- Static web system with all content hosted and documented
- Printed community map (A3, saddle-stitched, free distribution)
- Community contribution guide (so new stops can be added)
- Replication guide (so other city areas or other cities can duplicate it)

## Pilot 02 — The Healthy Tech Library (Creative Technology)

### The Brief

Install a dedicated creative technology space in Frankston Library. All tools are free, offline-capable, account-free, and ad-free.

This is a public art pilot because the outputs produced in the space — drawings, music, animations, writing — are creative works made by Frankston residents. The library becomes a studio.

### What Is Installed

A curated suite of creative software on dedicated library workstations, plus:
- One printed guide for each tool (laminated, at the station)
- One parent guide (take-home)
- One facilitator manual (for trained library staff)

No specialist hardware beyond standard library computers. No ongoing software licensing costs (all tools are open-source or free).

### Budget

| Line Item | Range |
|---|---|
| Tool evaluation and curation | $3,000–$5,000 |
| Space design and signage | $5,000–$8,000 |
| Facilitator training | $5,000–$8,000 |
| Print materials (guides, posters) | $2,000–$3,000 |
| Documentation | $5,000–$8,000 |
| Contingency | $5,000–$8,000 |
| **Total** | **$25,000–$40,000** |

*(Note: Library Creative Pack — the recommended first pilot — is a minimum viable version of Pilot 02. Cost: $20,000–$30,000.)*

### Deliverables

- Installed and operational creative technology space
- Trained library facilitators (minimum 3)
- Parent and community guide
- Facilitator manual
- Six-month program report
- Replication guide

## What All Three Pilots Share

Every pilot is governed by three non-negotiables:

**1. Permanent outcome.** Nothing in Frankston 2035 produces only an event or a report. Every pilot produces a physical or digital asset that exists after the funding ends.

**2. Community ownership.** Every asset is transferred to the community institution on completion. The school owns the mural. The library owns the space. The city owns the walk.

**3. Replication guide.** Every pilot produces a guide that allows another school, library, or city to replicate it without BSS involvement.

---

## Recommended Sequence

**Start here:** Library Creative Pack (reduced Pilot 02). $20,000–$30,000. 3–4 months. Single partner. Proves the model.

**Then:** QR Art Walk (Pilot 03). $20,000–$35,000. 6 months. Runs in parallel once Library pack is operational.

**Then:** Living Mural (Pilot 01). $45,000–$65,000. 12 months. Requires confirmed school relationship and First Nations protocol review complete.
