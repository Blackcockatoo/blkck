# Creative Operating System
## Frankston 2035 — How the System Works for Everyone

---

### What This Document Is

The project's invisible architecture. Every person who touches Frankston 2035 has a journey. These journeys are mapped here — individually and where they intersect.

Understanding the intersections is how you strengthen the system.

---

## The Creative Infrastructure Model

Frankston 2035 runs on five infrastructure layers:

```
LAYER 5 — CULTURE         (the why — civic identity, creative habit, belonging)
LAYER 4 — PROGRAMMING     (the what — workshops, walks, residencies, events)
LAYER 3 — SPACE           (the where — schools, library, streets, walls)
LAYER 2 — TOOLS           (the how — technology, materials, resources)
LAYER 1 — RELATIONSHIPS   (the foundation — community, artists, partners)
```

All visible work happens at layers 3–5.
All durable work happens at layers 1–2.

If a program runs beautifully but leaves no changed relationships and no better tools, it has added culture without adding infrastructure.

## The Seven Journeys

---

### Journey 1 — The Citizen

**Who:** A Frankston resident with no direct relationship to the project.

```
DISCOVERS
Something in the street — a mural, a QR code, a poster
        │
ENCOUNTERS
Scans a QR code. Walks past a mural. Hears about a program from a neighbour.
        │
PARTICIPATES
Attends a community workshop. Contributes a story. Votes on artwork design.
        │
IDENTIFIES
"This is about my city. This is about me."
        │
ADVOCATES
Tells someone else. Brings their family. Supports the next round.
        │
CONTRIBUTES
Volunteers, nominates artwork locations, shares local history.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- The first encounter must require nothing. No account, no sign-up, no commitment.
- The first encounter must be beautiful or surprising.
- The path from encounter to participation must be short and obvious.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Artist Journey at community workshops. Meets the Student Journey at school open days. Meets the Library Journey at public programs.

---

### Journey 2 — The Student

**Who:** A Frankston student, primary or secondary.

```
SEES
A mural goes up at their school. Or a teacher uses it in class.
        │
LEARNS
The mural is a lesson. Mathematics in the spiral. Language in the symbol.
        │
MAKES
Participates in the co-design of the next mural. Creates QR content.
        │
CONNECTS
Meets an artist. Visits the library's Healthy Tech space.
        │
IDENTIFIES
"I helped make something permanent in my school."
        │
GROWS
Carries the experience beyond the classroom.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- Students must be real co-designers, not just consultation checkboxes.
- The artwork must remain after the program ends.
- The student must be able to show a family member something that exists.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Teacher Journey at curriculum design. Meets the Artist Journey at the co-design workshop. Meets the Library Journey at creative technology programs.

---

### Journey 3 — The Teacher

**Who:** A Frankston teacher, primary or secondary.

```
HEARS
A colleague mentions the project. Or Council includes it in a professional development notice.
        │
INVESTIGATES
Reviews the curriculum resource pack. Attends an information session.
        │
PARTNERS
Nominates their class for a pilot program.
        │
FACILITATES
Uses the Living Mural as a teaching resource. Brings students to QR walk.
        │
ADVOCATES
Recommends the project to the school community and leadership.
        │
LEADS
Becomes a school-level champion. Trains other teachers.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- Teachers are overworked. Every interaction must respect their time.
- Resources must be ready-to-use, not requiring significant preparation.
- The link to the Victorian Curriculum must be clear and auditable.
- No platform they haven't used before. No accounts for students.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Student Journey in the classroom. Meets the Artist Journey at the co-design workshop. Meets the Library Journey at teacher professional development.

---

### Journey 4 — The Artist

**Who:** A local Frankston or Mornington Peninsula artist, any discipline.

```
HEARS
An open call, a word of mouth, a direct approach from BSS.
        │
APPLIES
Submits a portfolio and expression of interest.
        │
IS SELECTED
Commissioned for a specific project.
        │
CO-DESIGNS
Works with students, teachers, or community to develop the concept.
        │
CREATES
Produces the work. Is supported, not micro-managed.
        │
IS PAID
At professional rates. On time.
        │
IS CREDITED
Permanently, on the artwork and in all documentation.
        │
RETURNS
Invited to mentor, advise, or create again.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- Artists must be paid properly and treated as professionals.
- The co-design process must not compromise the artwork's integrity.
- The artist must know their name will be on this permanently.
- BSS never takes credit for an artist's work.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Student Journey at co-design. Meets the Citizen Journey at community workshops. Meets the Library Journey if residency-based.

---

### Journey 5 — The Library

**Who:** Frankston Library Service — staff, management, and the institution itself.

```
BRIEFED
Receives proposal for Healthy Tech Library module.
        │
SCOPED
Collaborates on what the space and programming will look like.
        │
TRAINED
Staff trained on tools, facilitation, Charter.
        │
LAUNCHES
Program opens to the public.
        │
RUNS
Ongoing programming. Regular community use.
        │
OWNS
The program is theirs. BSS steps back.
        │
EXPANDS
Pilot model extended to second library branch.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- The Library must feel like an owner, not a venue.
- Staff confidence with tools is non-negotiable before launch.
- The space must look and feel like it belongs in a library — not a tech showroom.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Citizen Journey at public programs. Meets the Student Journey at school holiday programs. Meets the Teacher Journey at professional development. Meets the Volunteer Journey at facilitation training.

---

### Journey 6 — The Volunteer

**Who:** A community member who wants to be more involved.

```
ENCOUNTERS
Attends a community event. Sees a call for facilitators.
        │
APPLIES
Expresses interest in supporting a program.
        │
TRAINED
Receives facilitation training from BSS or Library.
        │
FACILITATES
Runs sessions at the Library's Healthy Tech space.
        │
DEEPENS
Takes on more responsibility over time.
        │
TRAINS OTHERS
Becomes a trainer. The program scales through people, not budgets.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- Clear, bounded commitments. Not "whenever you can" — specific sessions.
- Real training. Volunteers are not free labour; they are community infrastructure.
- Recognition that is genuine, not performative.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Library Journey as facilitators. Meets the Citizen Journey as community advocates. Meets the Student Journey at youth programs.

---

### Journey 7 — The Council

**Who:** Frankston City Council — officers, Councillors, and the institution.

```
BRIEFED
Receives the Executive Summary and Council Ask.
        │
MEETS
45-minute conversation. No slide deck.
        │
AGREES
Partnership agreement signed.
        │
FACILITATES
Assists with permits, sites, venue access.
        │
FUNDS
Co-invests in pilots.
        │
MONITORS
Receives quarterly reports.
        │
CELEBRATES
Public launch events. Council name on permanent installations.
        │
EXPANDS
Recommends model to other Victorian councils.
```

**What makes this journey work:**
- Council must feel the project serves Frankston, not Blue Snake Studio.
- Reporting must be minimal, honest, and useful.
- Council's name appears on permanent outcomes, not just on funding acknowledgments.
- Council staff are partners, not administrators.

**Intersection points:** Meets the Citizen Journey at community events. Meets the Artist Journey through permit facilitation. Meets the Library Journey through institutional partnership.

## Intersection Map

Where journeys meet and what happens there:

| Intersection | Journeys | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Community co-design workshop | Citizen + Artist + Student | Artwork concept developed together |
| School mural launch | Student + Teacher + Artist + Citizen | Permanent artwork installed and celebrated |
| Library Healthy Tech program | Student + Volunteer + Library | Technology used for creation, not consumption |
| Teacher professional development | Teacher + Library + BSS | Curriculum resources embedded |
| QR Art Walk | Citizen + Artist + Community | Story of place told in public space |
| Council progress meeting | Council + BSS | Honest reporting, relationship deepened |
| Artist residency | Artist + Library + Student | Creative practice modelled in public |

## System Health Indicators

The system is working when:

- Artists are returning for second projects
- Teachers are requesting the program, not just accepting it
- Students can name the artist who made their school's mural
- Council officers mention the project without prompting
- Community members are suggesting new QR walk stops
- The Library program is full without BSS marketing it
- Volunteers are training other volunteers

The system is in trouble when:

- BSS is the only one talking about it
- Outcomes require BSS's continued presence to survive
- Any partner feels like a vendor, not a co-owner
