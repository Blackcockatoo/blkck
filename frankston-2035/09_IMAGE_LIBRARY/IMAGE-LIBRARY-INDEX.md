## Photography Direction

All Frankston 2035 photography must conform to the following principles (full guide: `../09-publication-design.md`):

- **No stock photography.** Ever. Every image is of a real person, place, or work.
- **Documentary style.** Natural light. Real environments. Unposed where possible.
- **Consent is non-negotiable.** Every image of a person requires written consent. Images of children require parental/guardian written consent. No exceptions.
- **Frankston is the subject.** Not a backdrop. The city is seen with interest and affection, not condescension.
- **No performed diversity.** Real community, genuinely documented.

## Section C — Rights and Usage Rules

| Scenario | Rule |
|---|---|
| Images of adults | Written consent required before publication |
| Images of children | Written parental/guardian consent required. Use school's existing consent form where possible. |
| Images of First Nations people or community | Additional cultural protocols apply. Follow `../06-first-nations-protocol.md`. Do not publish without explicit approval from BSS Creative Director. |
| BSS archive images | Assumed BSS ownership. Confirm per-image before any external publication. |
| Comparable project images from other cities | Always credit original source. Never present as Frankston-specific. |
| AI-generated imagery | Not permitted in any Frankston 2035 materials. All images must be real. |
| Stock photography | Not permitted in any Frankston 2035 materials. |

## What to Photograph First

If a photography session is scheduled, prioritise in this order:

1. Frankston Library exterior and interior (for Council pitch)
2. Frankston CBD and foreshore (for QR walk planning and vision materials)
3. Existing public art in Frankston LGA (for context and precedent)
4. A school classroom setting, with consent (for schools pack)

**Commission photography before the Council meeting**, even one session. A pitch with real Frankston images is materially stronger than one without.
