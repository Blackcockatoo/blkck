# Proof Register
## Frankston 2035 — Claims, Evidence, and Status

*Every major claim this project makes is listed here.*
*If a claim is not substantiated, it is marked as draft or proposal only.*
*No unsupported claim should appear in a Council-facing document.*

## How to Read This Register

**Status codes:**
- ✅ PROVEN — supported by evidence that can be cited or demonstrated
- 🔵 DRAFT — supported by reasonable inference or partial evidence
- ⚪ PROPOSAL — asserted but not yet tested or evidenced
- ⚠️ AT RISK — claim is made in public materials but evidence is weak

---

## Claim 1 — Murals can teach.

**The claim:** A mural embedded with curriculum content functions as a permanent teaching resource — not just decoration.

**Evidence / link:** Educational mural programs are well-documented internationally (see: Mural Arts Philadelphia curriculum program; Chicago Public Schools public art education initiatives). Victorian Curriculum links to visual art, mathematics, and language are established frameworks.

**Existing BSS project:** Studio artworks include curriculum-adjacent content (colouring pages, lyric sheets, sacred geometry linework). No school mural yet.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — methodology is sound, not yet tested in Frankston.

**Risk note:** If Pilot 01 produces a mural that is not used by teachers, this claim is undermined. Mitigate by making curriculum resource pack a non-negotiable deliverable, not optional.

---

## Claim 2 — QR codes can create community walks without apps, accounts, or tracking.

**The claim:** A self-guided creative walk can be delivered via QR codes linking to static web pages — no download, no account, no data collected.

**Evidence / link:** Static QR → static HTML is a proven technical model used widely. BSS has existing QR infrastructure deployed.

**Existing BSS project:** ✅ Multiple QR code campaigns in `/04_neon_venom/` and `/17_branding_campaigns/` — QR codes linking to content pages exist and function. `neon_venom_qr_enter_the_dream_v1.png`, `woman-money-venom-full-qr.png` etc. are live examples.

**Status:** ✅ PROVEN — QR delivery model works. Walk format is new but technically trivial.

**Risk note:** Content quality is the variable. The technical model is sound; the community engagement process to gather content is unproven.

---

## Claim 3 — Healthy technology can be delivered in a library setting without accounts or data collection.

**The claim:** Offline-capable, account-free creative tools exist, work in a library context, and are usable by children without facilitation specialisation.

**Evidence / link:** Open-source creative tools (Krita, Audacity, Scratch offline, LMMS, OpenShot) are all free, offline-capable, account-free. Public libraries internationally have implemented similar programs (see: Toronto Public Library MakerSpace model; Auckland Libraries digital creation spaces).

**Existing BSS project:** Meta-Pet concept (offline-first, privacy-respecting, child-facing) establishes BSS's philosophy and technical understanding. Teacher Meta-Pet pack demonstrates educator-facing thinking.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — tools exist and have been identified. Library implementation not yet scoped in Frankston.

**Risk note:** Staff training is the risk. Tools that are technically functional may be intimidating for library staff without facilitation support. Mitigate with structured training.

---

## Claim 4 — Public art increases community belonging.

**The claim:** Commissioning and installing public art in Frankston will increase residents' sense of local identity and pride.

**Evidence / link:** Placemaking research is extensive (see: Project for Public Spaces; PPS "What Makes a Great Place"; Australian Government Place-Based Investment Framework documentation; Victorian Government Creative Suburbs program outcomes).

**Existing BSS project:** Frankston-specific BSS work includes `/20_moss_man/moss-man-frankston-courts.png` and `/18_frankston_fuji/` materials — both demonstrating local place engagement. No community survey data yet.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — claim is well-supported in literature, not yet measured in Frankston.

**Risk note:** Community belonging is not guaranteed — a project that the community feels was done *to* them rather than *with* them can decrease belonging. Co-design is the mitigation.

---

## Claim 5 — Students who co-design public art have a stronger relationship with their school environment.

**The claim:** Participation in the design of a permanent school mural increases students' connection to the school.

**Evidence / link:** Participatory design with young people in school contexts is supported by research (see: OECD "Student Agency for 2030"; Victorian Government Student Voice and Agency framework; Arts in Education Ireland impact studies). Direct mural co-design research is limited but consistent.

**Existing BSS project:** No school co-design project yet.

**Status:** ⚪ PROPOSAL — reasonable inference from adjacent evidence. Not yet tested.

**Risk note:** If the co-design process is performative (students consulted but not heard), the opposite outcome occurs. One non-negotiable: at least one student-originated element must appear in the final artwork unchanged.

---

## Claim 6 — The First Nations protocol protects the project from cultural risk.

**The claim:** Following the 9-part protocol (document 06) significantly reduces the risk of inadvertent cultural harm or misrepresentation.

**Evidence / link:** Protocol follows best-practice frameworks for Australian arts organisations (see: Reconciliation Australia; Arts Law Centre of Australia First Nations cultural protocols guidance; Victorian Traditional Owner Cultural Heritage Working Group resources).

**Existing BSS project:** The 06 series — nine documents including full treatment, purpose statement, council version, bullet version, memetic version, visual layout, required assets, and next actions.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — protocol is well-constructed but has not been reviewed by a cultural safety advisor (mandatory before operational use). Until that review occurs, the claim is unconfirmed.

**Risk note:** A protocol is not protection in itself. Only following the protocol provides protection. The protocol review must happen before any engagement.

## Claim 7 — This model is replicable by other councils.

**The claim:** Once the Frankston pilots are documented, another Australian council could implement the model without Blue Snake Studio's direct involvement.

**Evidence / link:** Design for replicability is built into every pilot (documentation requirements, replication guides, transferred assets). Open licensing is planned. No other council has adopted it yet.

**Existing BSS project:** BSS produces packs (colouring pages, lyric sheets, teacher resources) designed for others to use without BSS involvement. The pack model is an existing capability.

**Status:** ⚪ PROPOSAL — replicability is designed for, not yet demonstrated. Cannot be proven until the first pilot is complete and documented.

**Risk note:** Do not claim replicability in Council materials until at least one pilot has been documented and the documentation tested by a third party.

## Claim 8 — The budget estimates are realistic.

**The claim:** The three pilots can be delivered within the stated budget ranges.

| Pilot | Estimate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Library Creative Pack | $20,000–$30,000 | Tool costs (open-source = $0 software), facilitation, installation, documentation |
| Living Mural | $45,000–$65,000 | Artist fee ($25–35k), materials ($5–8k), facilitation ($5–10k), documentation ($5k) |
| QR Art Walk | $20,000–$35,000 | Content creation ($10–15k), web build ($3–5k), print ($2–3k), installation ($2–4k), documentation ($3–5k) |

**Evidence:** Artist fee ranges benchmarked against NAVA (National Association for the Visual Arts) recommended rates. Material costs based on standard public art project benchmarks. Facilitation and documentation costs from BSS project history.

**Existing BSS project:** BSS has produced QR campaigns, packs, and community art materials within comparable cost ranges.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — estimates are informed and include 15% contingency. Not yet validated against supplier quotes.

**Risk note:** Artist fees are the highest variable. NAVA rates are a guide, not a market rate. Get competitive quotes before committing to Council.

## Claim 9 — Frankston has community appetite for this.

**The claim:** There is sufficient community interest in Frankston to sustain creative participation programs.

**Evidence / link:** 2021 ABS Census data: Frankston LGA has a growing young population (under-18 share above Victorian average in outer metro areas). Frankston Arts Centre attendance data (available via Council). Frankston Library loan and visit data.

**Existing BSS project:** BSS's `18_frankston_fuji` folder contains Frankston-Susono community music and language materials — evidence of existing community-facing work in Frankston.

**Status:** 🔵 DRAFT — community appetite is inferred from demographics and existing Council cultural programs, not yet measured directly.

**Risk note:** Don't claim "community demand" without evidence. Frame as "community need supported by demographic data" until a community engagement process confirms appetite.

## Claim 10 — Blue Snake Studio can deliver this.

**The claim:** BSS has the capability to design, manage, and document the proposed pilots.

**Evidence:**

| Capability required | BSS evidence |
|---|---|
| Public art concept and production | Extensive visual art portfolio (24 print editions, paintings, murals, branding) |
| Community engagement facilitation | BSS community programs, teacher packs, outreach materials |
| QR and web delivery | Existing QR campaigns, static web pages, site architecture |
| Educational resource design | Teacher Meta-Pet pack, colouring page packs, lyric sheets |
| Documentation and knowledge transfer | Content documentation: `CODEX_PROMPT.md`, `GALLERY_ROOMS.md`, `SITE_MAP.md` etc. |
| Healthy technology understanding | Meta-Pet (offline-first, account-free, child-safe design) |

**Status:** ✅ PROVEN — across individual capabilities. Project management at Frankston 2035 scale is new but grounded in demonstrated component skills.

**Risk note:** Scale risk is real. A pilot system across Council, Library, and School simultaneously is more complex than any single BSS project to date. Mitigate by running one pilot at a time, not three in parallel.
