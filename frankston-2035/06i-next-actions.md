## Phase 1 — Before Any Engagement Begins
*These must be complete before any First Nations contact is initiated.*

---

### 1.1 — Protocol Review by Cultural Safety Advisor

**Action:** Engage an independent Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander cultural safety advisor to review document 06 (full protocol).

**Steps:**
- [ ] Contact Creative Victoria's First Nations arts team to request a referral (not a cold approach)
- [ ] Alternatively: contact Regional Arts Victoria for their recommendation
- [ ] Brief the advisor clearly: this is a review of an internal protocol, not content review
- [ ] Send document 06 and document 06b (Purpose Statement)
- [ ] Confirm fee arrangement before engagement (budget $1,500–$3,000)
- [ ] Receive written feedback
- [ ] Incorporate feedback into protocol v3.0
- [ ] Note: protocol remains internal-use-only until this step is complete

**Trigger:** Start immediately. No other Phase 1 actions are blocked by this one — run in parallel.
**Estimated time to complete:** 4–8 weeks (dependent on advisor availability).

---

### 1.2 — Legal Review of Permission Document Template

**Action:** Submit the permission document template (Appendix D of the Playbook) to the Arts Law Centre of Australia for review.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Log query at artslaw.com.au
- [ ] Submit the template with context: civic arts project, non-commercial, First Nations cultural permissions
- [ ] Receive and incorporate feedback
- [ ] Updated template replaces Appendix D in the Playbook

**Trigger:** Start immediately. Independent of 1.1.
**Estimated time to complete:** 2–6 weeks (Arts Law Centre has a queue).

---

### 1.3 — Project Research Summary

**Action:** Produce a written summary of publicly available information relevant to First Nations context in Frankston.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Review publicly available information about the Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal Corporation
- [ ] Access the Aboriginal Victoria Heritage Register — search for registered sites in Frankston LGA
- [ ] Review any existing CHMPs for proposed project sites (Council planning department may have these)
- [ ] Research arts organisations and community services with First Nations relationships in Frankston area
- [ ] Draft summary document and save to project records
- [ ] Note: this research informs our approach — it does not constitute cultural intelligence or permission

**Trigger:** Start immediately. Complete before 2.1.
**Estimated time to complete:** 2–3 weeks.

---

### 1.4 — Intermediary Mapping

**Action:** Identify organisations or individuals who may be able to make a respectful introduction.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Contact Frankston City Council cultural heritage team to ask whether they have existing First Nations relationships or contacts
- [ ] Research Peninsula Health Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Team
- [ ] Research Local Aboriginal Education Consultative Groups (LAECGs) connected to Frankston schools
- [ ] Contact Koorie Heritage Trust for advice (they may not be able to help directly but can advise)
- [ ] Document findings: who, what relationship, contact, how to approach
- [ ] Do not make any First Nations contact at this stage — this is mapping only

**Trigger:** Start after 1.3 is underway. Complete before 2.1.
**Estimated time to complete:** 2–4 weeks.

---

**Steps:**

### 1.6 — Team Protocol Briefing

**Action:** Brief all BSS personnel working on Frankston 2035 on the protocol.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Schedule 60–90 minute session
- [ ] Use 06d (Bullet Point Version) and 06f (Memetic Version) as reference materials
- [ ] Cover: what the protocol commits us to, what each person must and must not do, how to log decisions and concerns, what to do if something goes wrong

**Trigger:** Complete before any Phase 2 actions. Cannot brief after first contact has already been made.

## Phase 2 — Before First Contact

*Complete all of Phase 1 before starting Phase 2.*
*Phase 2 actions prepare us to initiate relationship — not to seek anything from it.*

---

### 2.1 — First Contact Strategy

**Action:** Based on 1.3 (Research) and 1.4 (Intermediary Mapping), determine the most appropriate path to first contact.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Review research and intermediary mapping
- [ ] Identify the most appropriate first point of contact (organisation, not individual, where possible)
- [ ] Identify the intermediary who will make the introduction
- [ ] Draft the introduction request: who we are, what we're trying to do, a request for a conversation — nothing more
- [ ] Have the draft reviewed internally before sending
- [ ] Confirm the introduction approach with the intermediary
- [ ] Do not send without intermediary support if at all possible

**Trigger:** All Phase 1 steps complete (or in progress — 1.1 and 1.2 may still be underway, but 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6 must be done).
**Note:** If cultural safety advisor review (1.1) has not yet returned feedback, note this in the decision log and proceed with caution — first contact is a request for a conversation only and does not trigger cultural safety requirements.

---

### 2.2 — Site Cultural Significance Assessment

**Action:** Before any site is finalised for a pilot, complete a cultural significance assessment.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Pilot 01 (Mural school): confirm proposed school site and conduct heritage search
- [ ] Pilot 03 (QR Walk): map all proposed walk stops and conduct heritage search for each
- [ ] For each site: check Victorian Heritage Register, Council heritage databases, Aboriginal Victoria Heritage Register
- [ ] Document findings for each site in project records
- [ ] If significance identified or uncertain: do not proceed with that site without community advice

**Trigger:** As pilot sites are being considered. Complete before any site is confirmed.

## Phase 3 — Ongoing Operational

*These actions run throughout the project lifecycle.*

---

### 3.1 — Annual Protocol Review

**Action:** Review the protocol each June against what happened in the preceding twelve months.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Review decision log
- [ ] Review any contact made with First Nations individuals or organisations
- [ ] Review any cultural safety concerns raised
- [ ] Review any breaches and how they were handled
- [ ] Seek feedback from any First Nations contacts (with their consent)
- [ ] Update the protocol as required
- [ ] Document the review in the decision log

**First review due:** June 2027.

---

### 3.2 — Cultural Safety Audit — Per Pilot

**Action:** Before any publicly visible artwork or installation is finalised, commission a cultural safety review.

**Steps for each pilot:**
- [ ] Identify a qualified cultural safety advisor (may be the same advisor engaged in 1.1 or a different person)
- [ ] Commission written review covering: site, visual content, narrative, consultation process, documentation
- [ ] Receive and incorporate feedback before finalising
- [ ] Retain the audit report permanently in project records
- [ ] If significant concerns are identified: do not proceed until resolved

**Trigger:** Before any artwork concept is approved for production.

---

### 3.3 — Rapid Response Protocol

**Action:** Ensure the Creative Director can respond within 48 hours to any cultural concern raised about a completed artwork.

**Steps:**
- [ ] Creative Director nominates a deputy (if applicable) who can respond if Creative Director is unavailable
- [ ] Create a simple response template: "We have received your concern. We are taking it seriously. We will respond substantively by [date]."
- [ ] Ensure all completed artworks have a visible attribution and contact point
- [ ] Document any concern received and every response in the decision log

**Trigger:** This capability is needed from the moment the first pilot is installed.
