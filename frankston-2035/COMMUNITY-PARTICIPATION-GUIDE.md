# Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
## Community Participation Guide

*For: Frankston residents, community groups, local businesses, and anyone who wants to be part of this.*

---

## What This Project Is

Frankston 2035 is putting permanent art into the city's schools, libraries, and streets.

Not commissioned from outside. Not designed by committee. Not placed by a developer to meet a planning condition.

Made here. By people who live here. For the city they live in.

This guide explains how you can be part of it.

---

## Five Ways to Participate

### 1. Nominate a Place

The QR Art Walk needs sites — places in Frankston that have a story worth telling.

A wall where something happened. A corner where you always stop. A view that people from outside never see. A place your grandparent talked about.

**How to nominate:**
Send an email to bluesssnakestudio@gmail.com with:
- The location (street address or description)
- One sentence about why it matters
- Your name (or stay anonymous — both are fine)

Nominated sites are reviewed for the walk route. We cannot promise every site will be included, but every nomination is read and considered.

---

### 2. Contribute Artwork or a Story

The QR Art Walk is built from local voices. We are looking for:

- Original artwork (any medium — digital, painted, drawn, photographed)
- Short writing (poetry, personal essays, place descriptions — 50–300 words)
- Audio recordings (music, spoken word, oral history — 1–5 minutes)
- Photography (of places, not necessarily people)

**What we need from you:**
- The work itself, in digital form
- A one-paragraph bio or statement
- Written confirmation that the work is yours to contribute

**What you receive:**
- Full credit in the walk materials (both print and digital)
- A permanent place in the walk (content is not removed after the project ends)
- A contributor copy of the printed walk map

**Rights:** You keep full ownership of your work. We ask for the right to display it as part of the walk and to reproduce it in project documentation. We will never sell your work or use it for purposes you haven't agreed to.

**How to submit:** bluesssnakestudio@gmail.com — subject line: "Walk Contribution"

---

### 3. Tell Your School About the Mural

If you have children at a Frankston primary school, or if you work at one, pass on the Schools Introduction Pack.

The Living Mural needs a school — one school, to start. The school that says yes first becomes part of something permanent. Their students' names go on the wall. Their teachers help shape what it teaches. The mural stays when everyone has moved on.

**For teachers and principals:** See `04_SCHOOLS_AND_LEARNING/SCHOOLS-INTRODUCTION-PACK.md`

---

### 4. Use the Library Creative Space

When the Healthy Tech Library opens, it will be free and open to everyone during library hours.

No booking. No account. No class to enrol in. Just a space where you can sit down and make something.

Drawing software. Music tools. Writing programs. Video editing. All free. All offline. All yours.

**When will it open?** Once Council endorses the project and the Library Service confirms the partnership. Sign up to hear when: bluesssnakestudio@gmail.com — subject line: "Notify me"

---

### 5. Support the Project

If you believe in what this project is trying to do:

**Tell people.** Word of mouth from people who live in Frankston is more powerful than any press release.

**Write to Council.** A short email to your local Councillor expressing support for the project carries real weight. You don't need to say much — "I support Frankston 2035 and think Council should get behind it" is enough.

**Local businesses:** If your business wants to support the project through sponsorship or in-kind contribution, get in touch. We are not looking for naming rights or brand placement. We are looking for people who want to be part of building something real.

## What This Is Not

**It is not a consultation exercise.** We are not asking for community input to tick a box. Everything contributed is genuinely used or genuinely considered.

**It is not a fundraiser.** We are not asking Frankston residents for money. We are asking for your stories, your art, your voice, and your support.

**It is not temporary.** Every outcome of Frankston 2035 is designed to be permanent. The mural stays on the wall. The walk stays on the street. The library space keeps running. Your contribution stays.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to be an artist to contribute?**
No. The walk needs voices, stories, and places — not just visual art. If you have something worth saying about a place in Frankston, that is enough.

**What if I don't feel confident in my writing or art?**
Submit anyway. We edit with care. If something isn't right for the walk, we'll tell you honestly and explain why — not just reject without a reason.

**Can community groups participate?**
Yes. If you represent a Frankston community group — a sporting club, a cultural organisation, a neighbourhood house, a community garden — contact us to discuss how your group could contribute a stop or a story.

**What if I have a concern about the project?**
Email us. We will respond. If your concern is about the First Nations protocol, the cultural safety process, or anything related to cultural representation, we take those seriously and will escalate to the appropriate advisor.

**Is this a government project?**
No. Frankston 2035 is run by Blue Snake Studio, an independent creative practice. We are seeking partnership with Frankston City Council, but the project is not a Council program. BSS is responsible for the content and approach.
