## The Project in One Paragraph

Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives is a ten-year civic creativity initiative placing permanent public art in Frankston schools, healthy creative technology in Frankston Library, and a community-built art walk through the city's streets. Run by Blue Snake Studio, it begins with three pilots costing $95,000–$150,000 combined — seeking co-investment from Frankston City Council and matched grant funding. Every outcome is designed to be owned by the community and to last beyond the project.

## The Three Pilots

### Pilot 01 — The Living Mural
A large-scale mural co-designed with students in a Frankston primary school. Curriculum-linked. Permanent. Owned by the school. Delivered with a curriculum resource pack that teachers can use in class.

**Timeline:** 12 months from commission
**Budget:** $45,000–$65,000

### Pilot 02 — The Healthy Tech Library
A dedicated creative technology space in Frankston Library. Drawing, music, writing, animation — all using free, offline, account-free software. No tracking. No advertising. The space belongs to the Library on completion.

**Timeline:** 6 months setup, then ongoing
**Budget:** $30,000–$50,000

*(The first step is the Library Creative Pack — a minimum viable version of Pilot 02 at $20,000–$30,000, recommended as the first pilot.)*

### Pilot 03 — The QR Art Walk
A self-guided creative walk through Frankston's CBD and foreshore. QR codes open local stories, artworks, and audio. No app required. No account. Content created by local artists and community members. The walk belongs to the city.

**Timeline:** 6 months development, then permanent
**Budget:** $20,000–$35,000

## Key Messages

**On the project:**
"Frankston has creative people. What it lacks is creative infrastructure — the systems that connect individual acts of creativity to civic life. That's what we're building."

**On permanence:**
"Every other creative program in this city produces an event. Frankston 2035 produces infrastructure. When the funding ends, the mural is still on the wall."

**On technology:**
"We ask three questions of every piece of technology before it enters any program: Does it serve the child, not the corporation? Does it work without an account? Does it work offline? If any answer is no, the technology does not enter the program."

**On scale:**
"We're not asking Council to fund a ten-year vision. We're asking them to co-invest in one shelf of tools in a library. If it works — and it will — that's the proof we need for everything else."

**On ownership:**
"Blue Snake Studio is the engine of this project. Frankston is the hero. We leave at the end. The art stays."

## About Blue Snake Studio

Blue Snake Studio is a Frankston-rooted creative practice working across public art, community programs, and educational resources.

Existing work includes QR-based community content campaigns, educational resource packs for teachers and families, bilingual cultural materials, and public art concepts developed specifically for Frankston. BSS is the designer, coordinator, and document of the Frankston 2035 program. It does not seek naming rights, ownership of community assets, or ongoing management fees.
