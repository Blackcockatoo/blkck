# First Nations Protocol — Memetic Version
## Frankston 2035 — Key Statements for High-Impact Communication

*Distilled principles for internal training, public communication, conference presentations, and team briefings.*
*Every statement here is about process — never about cultural content.*
*Not for use in any First Nations engagement context without advisor review.*

---

## The Core Truth

**"The first step is not content creation. The first step is respectful process."**

---

## On Honesty

"We are outside. We say that plainly. We proceed from there."

"Honesty is the only foundation a relationship can be built on."

"If a funder asks whether we have community support, we say the truth: we are building towards it."

"The disrespect has usually not come from malice. It has come from speed, assumption, and the belief that a good outcome justifies a compromised process."

"We start where we are. We proceed slowly. We go only as far as we are welcomed."

---

## On Process

"The sequence is not optional. Relationship before project — always."

"We do not manufacture relationships."

"No timeline created by a funder overrides the pace of genuine relationship."

"If the relationship is not established when the launch date arrives, the launch date moves."

"Process is part of the outcome. There is no good creative outcome that justifies a compromised process."

---

## On Permission

"Permission is specific, not general."

"Silence is not permission. Partial engagement is not permission. One person's yes is not a community's yes."

"Permission to have a conversation is not permission to use cultural material."

"Permission from one community member is not permission from the community."

"Verbal permission does not cover public use. It never does."

"We allow weeks, not hours. We say: there is no hurry."

---

## On Refusal

"No means no. It does not mean try again differently."

"We do not ask a different person the same question hoping for a different answer."

"We bear the cost of withdrawal. Always."

"There is no creative direction, no public commitment, and no funding condition that overrides a refusal."

"We receive refusals without negotiating them."

---

## On Acknowledgement

"Acknowledgement of Country is appropriate. It is not engagement."

"You cannot Acknowledge your way past the work of relationship."

"An Acknowledgement performed at the opening of a meeting and then forgotten during the meeting is not cultural safety. It is theatre."

---

## On Cultural Safety

"Cultural safety is designed in — not added at the end."

"We do not use this protocol as a shield — citing its existence as evidence of cultural safety without doing the actual work."

"A document does not constitute a relationship."

"Cultural safety is not cultural avoidance. The goal is not a project scrubbed clean of First Nations reference. The goal is a project in which every reference is made with care, permission, and honesty."

---

## On Accountability

"A written protocol without accountability is a piece of paper."

"Breaches are reported transparently — not managed quietly."

"We do not use this protocol as a shield."

"The quality of our First Nations relationships is not measured by this document. It is measured by whether First Nations people in Frankston, over time, describe Blue Snake Studio as an organisation that kept its word. That is the only measure that matters."

---

## On Patience

"Patience in this context is not passive waiting. It is active respect."

"Impatience is itself a form of disrespect."

"We do not follow up a first contact more than once. Silence is information."

---

## The One-Sentence Version

"Before we create anything, we listen. Before we listen, we earn the right to be in the room."

---

## For Team Briefings

When briefing new team members, partners, or collaborators, these three statements cover the essentials:

1. "Everything we do in Frankston is on Country. That changes what we have to do before we can do anything else."

2. "The first step is not content creation. The first step is respectful process. The sequence matters and cannot be compressed."

3. "We have no existing First Nations relationships in Frankston. We say so plainly. That is where we start."
