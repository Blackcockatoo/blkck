## What This Could Be

Frankston and Susono have been sister cities since 1981.

That relationship — decades old — lives mostly in formal civic ceremony. It has not, to date, produced lasting creative exchange between communities, schools, or artists.

Frankston 2035 proposes to change that. Not through a formal delegation or an exchange tour. Through something that can be used in classrooms, shared between families, and added to over years: a bilingual creative resource pack.

---

## The Proposal: A Bilingual Creative Classroom Pack

A print and digital resource designed for use in Frankston primary schools and Susono primary schools.

The pack connects both cities through creative language learning, shared local stories, and parallel artistic traditions.

### What the Pack Contains

**Language Layer**
- Everyday Japanese phrases taught through visual art rather than rote learning
- Everyday English phrases formatted equivalently for Susono students
- Simple illustrated vocabulary: colours, shapes, sounds, places, foods, feelings
- Each page designed so both sides of the exchange learn from the same image

**Cultural Layer**
- Visual art exercises drawing on both cities' landscapes (Frankston foreshore / Suruga Bay coast; Mornington Peninsula flora / Mount Fuji foothills flora)
- Music activities drawing on Japanese traditional forms and contemporary Australian forms — parallel, not merged
- Oral history prompts: "What does your grandparent say about this city?"

**Exchange Layer**
- One artwork per class, sent digitally to a partner school in the other city
- One letter per student (in their own language — not translated — with a simple visual illustration)
- One shared creative prompt per term, translated into both languages

### What the Pack Does Not Do

- Does not claim cultural authority over Japanese traditions or art forms
- Does not merge or conflate the two cultures
- Does not require Japan-specific expertise from Frankston teachers or English-specific expertise from Susono teachers
- Does not require any technology beyond a printer and a postage stamp (digital exchange is an add-on, not a requirement)

## What Blue Snake Studio Has Already Built

BSS has produced Frankston–Susono bilingual creative materials as part of previous work. These include:

- Bilingual lyric sheets connecting Frankston and Susono music traditions
- Illustrated language reference sheets
- Community-facing cultural materials in both English and Japanese

These materials are in the BSS archive. They are the proof of concept for this pack.

## What Has to Happen Before This Proceeds

In sequence:

1. **Frankston City Council confirms** that the sister-cities program is an active priority and that there is a Council officer with responsibility for the Susono relationship. Without this, the project has no institutional anchor on the Australian side.

2. **Council facilitates an introduction** to the relevant Susono municipal or educational body. BSS does not approach Susono independently.

3. **A Susono counterpart is identified and agrees** — a school, an arts organisation, or a community group that wants to participate.

4. **A simple bilateral agreement** is drafted — not a formal treaty, but a document that both sides have read and agreed to. BSS prepares a draft; both cities review.

5. **A pilot classroom on each side is identified.** One Frankston primary class. One Susono primary class. The exchange starts there.

Only then does BSS begin producing the full pack.

## What Both Cities Receive

**Frankston:**
- A curriculum-linked resource used in multiple primary schools
- A living connection to a sister city that students have actually contributed to
- A bilingual creative pack freely available to all Frankston schools

**Susono:**
- The same resource, translated, for use in Susono schools
- Original creative works from Frankston students
- A documented exchange program they can continue independently
