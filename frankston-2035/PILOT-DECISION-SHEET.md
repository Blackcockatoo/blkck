## Recommendation

**Run the Library Creative Pack first.**

Score: 26/27. Highest across all five options.

### Why

**It is the fastest to complete.** 3–4 months from go to done. Council can see a result before the financial year ends.

**It is the cheapest.** $20,000–$30,000. Council's contribution could be as low as $12,000 with BSS matching.

**It has the fewest dependencies.** One partner (Library Service). No artist to commission. No school to navigate. No software to build.

**It has zero First Nations cultural risk.** No cultural content is involved. The Healthy Technology Charter governs everything in the space.

**It demonstrates the core philosophy immediately.** A shelf of offline creative tools in a public library is the Screen as Doorway concept made physical. Parents can see it. Children can use it. Council can point to it.

**It builds the Library relationship.** Pilot 02 (Healthy Tech Library, full version) requires a deep Library partnership. Pilot 0 (the Library Creative Pack) is how you earn that relationship before asking for more.

**It is replicable day one.** Once the pack is designed and the tools are curated, any library in Victoria can duplicate it. The documentation becomes freely available.

### Why Not the Others

**Living Mural QR Layer:** Strong, but needs 8–12 months and a school relationship that does not yet exist. Run this second.

**Healthy Tech Mini-App:** Software development is the highest-risk activity in any project. A custom app has no minimum viable shortcut. Defer indefinitely until pilots 1 and 2 are proven.

**Frankston × Susono Pack:** The Susono partnership is proposed, not confirmed. Until sister-city exchange is formally on the table through Council's program, this remains a concept. Do not proceed without confirmed partnership.

**Respect Through Participation:** This is a process pilot, not a product pilot. Its output is documentation — not something Council can point to publicly. It has high intrinsic value but low Council demonstration value. Run it in parallel with other pilots as the First Nations protocol pathway advances — not as the headline first pilot.

## What Happens After the Library Creative Pack

**Month 4–6:** Library Creative Pack running. Document the process. Write the replication guide.

**Month 6–9:** Begin Frankston × Susono Pack (if sister-city relationship confirmed by Council). Begin scoping Living Mural QR Layer with schools.

**Month 9–12:** Living Mural QR Layer in production. Library partnership deepened for Pilot 02.

**Month 12–18:** Full Healthy Tech Library (Pilot 02) scoped and launched. Living Mural complete.
