# Frankston 2035 — Art That Lives
## Schools and Teachers Introduction Pack

*For: Primary school principals, teachers, and curriculum coordinators in Frankston*
*Prepared by: Blue Snake Studio*

---

## What Is This Project?

Frankston 2035 is a creative infrastructure program for Frankston — one that starts in schools.

We are looking for one Frankston primary school to be the site of the first permanent public artwork in the program: a large-scale mural, co-designed with your students, curriculum-linked, and owned by your school forever.

We are also working with Frankston Library to bring healthy, offline-first creative technology tools to young people — drawing, music, writing, and animation software that works without accounts and without tracking.

---

## The Living Mural — What We Are Offering

### What It Is

A large-scale mural — typically on an exterior wall or in a high-traffic indoor space — designed in collaboration with your students and painted by a professional artist.

This is not a decorative commission. The visual content of the mural is built around curriculum themes your teachers identify. The artwork teaches.

### What Your School Gets

- One permanent, large-scale mural — owned by the school, no ongoing cost
- One curriculum resource pack, developed with your teachers, that links the mural to learning areas including Visual Arts, Mathematics, and English
- A documented co-design process that your students were part of
- A credited, professional artist who spends time in your school
- A replication guide so other schools can follow your example

### What It Costs the School

**Nothing financial.** Blue Snake Studio coordinates the project. Frankston City Council facilitates site access and permits. Funding is sought from Creative Victoria and Australia Council.

**What we ask from the school:**

- One teacher or coordinator nominated as the school's point of contact (est. 2–4 hours total)
- Two to three curriculum planning sessions with your nominated teacher (1 hour each)
- One or two sessions where students participate in the co-design process (in class, facilitated by BSS)
- Written permission from the principal for the installation
- Willingness to use the curriculum resource pack in class

### The Timeline

| Stage | Time | Who Does It |
|---|---|---|
| School selected and briefed | Month 0 | BSS + Council |
| Curriculum planning sessions | Months 1–2 | BSS + nominated teacher |
| Student co-design sessions | Month 2 | BSS in your classroom |
| Artist selected and briefed | Month 3 | BSS |
| Design developed and approved | Months 3–4 | Artist + BSS + school |
| Mural installed | Months 5–8 | Artist (on-site) |

## Healthy Technology in Schools

### What the Healthy Technology Charter Says

Every piece of technology used in Frankston 2035 programs must answer yes to three questions:

1. Does it serve the child, not the corporation?
2. Does it work without an account?
3. Does it work offline, or can it?

If any answer is no, the technology does not enter the program.

### What This Means in Practice

All software used in any Frankston 2035 program is:
- **Free** — no cost to school or family
- **Account-free** — no student creates a login
- **Offline-capable** — works without internet
- **Ad-free** — no advertising, ever
- **Open-source or privacy-respecting** — code is transparent or independently audited

### Tools We Use and Recommend

| Tool | What It Does | Age Range |
|---|---|---|
| Krita | Professional digital drawing and painting | 8+ |
| Scratch (offline) | Visual programming and interactive stories | 7–14 |
| Audacity | Audio recording and editing | 10+ |
| LMMS | Music composition and production | 10+ |
| OpenShot | Video editing | 12+ |

All tools are free to download and install on school computers. BSS provides installation guidance and facilitator training.

## The Curriculum Connection

The Living Mural is designed to connect to the Victorian Curriculum across multiple learning areas.

| Curriculum area | Connection |
|---|---|
| Visual Arts — Levels 3–6 | Students explore visual language, composition, and meaning-making in the co-design process |
| Mathematics — Geometry | Scale, proportion, symmetry, and spatial reasoning in mural design |
| English — Language | Reading the mural as a text; writing responses and reflections |
| Humanities — Civics and Citizenship | Public art as civic expression; Frankston's identity and history |
| Cross-curriculum: Critical and Creative Thinking | Co-design process develops student agency and iterative thinking |

A full curriculum resource pack — including lesson plans, assessment frameworks, and student activity sheets — is developed in collaboration with your teachers and delivered before the mural is complete.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who chooses the design?**
The design emerges from the co-design process. Students generate ideas. The artist synthesises them. BSS and the school approve the final design. No element is imposed from outside.

**Can we have input into the subject matter?**
Yes. The curriculum link means the subject matter is partly determined by what your teachers want to teach. This is one of the first questions we ask.

**What if our students or community are sensitive to certain themes?**
We follow a careful community consent process. Nothing controversial appears in a school mural without full school and parent notification. The First Nations engagement process is documented and rigorous.

**Is the mural permanent?**
Yes. Once installed, the mural is the school's property. It requires standard exterior maintenance (the artist specifies the paint system). BSS has no ongoing role after delivery.

**What if we want to change the mural later?**
The school owns it. That is a decision for the school to make.

**Will our students be photographed?**
Only with explicit parental consent, using the school's existing photography permission framework.
