# Pilot Projects
## Frankston 2035 — Three Proving Grounds

---

### Purpose of the Pilots

Three pilots. Three different settings. One shared purpose: prove that the model works before we scale it.

Each pilot is designed to:
- Deliver a permanent, community-owned outcome
- Generate replicable documentation
- Build at least one relationship that did not exist before
- Leave the next person something to build on

---

## Pilot 01 — The Living Mural

**Where:** A Frankston primary school (location TBC with Council)
**Duration:** 12 months from commission to installation
**Lead:** Blue Snake Studio, in partnership with school and selected artist

### What It Is

A large-scale mural on a school building, designed in direct collaboration with students, linked to at least one area of the Victorian Curriculum, and maintained as a living classroom resource.

### What Makes It Different

The mural is not decoration. It contains curriculum content — mathematics, science, language, history — embedded into its visual language. Teachers receive a resource pack. Students participate in the design process. The artist becomes a temporary resident educator.

### Outcomes

- One permanent public artwork
- One curriculum resource pack (freely licensed)
- One documented co-design process (replicable model)
- One school with a changed relationship to creative practice

### Budget Indicative Range

$45,000–$65,000 (artist fee, materials, facilitation, documentation)

## Pilot 02 — The Healthy Tech Library

**Where:** Frankston Library
**Duration:** 6 months setup, ongoing programming
**Lead:** Blue Snake Studio, in partnership with Frankston Library Service

### What It Is

A dedicated module within Frankston Library — physical and programmatic — that offers screen-healthy, privacy-respecting, creative technology experiences for children and young people.

### What It Contains

- Offline creative tools (music, drawing, building, writing)
- No accounts, no data collection, no advertising
- A curated selection of open-source educational software
- A facilitator program (trained volunteers and library staff)
- A parent/carer information resource

### What Makes It Different

This is not a computer lab. It is a creative technology commons. Every tool in it has been evaluated against the Healthy Technology Charter (see document 04). Nothing extracts data from children.

### Outcomes

- A physical installation in Frankston Library
- A Healthy Technology Charter adopted by the Library
- A facilitator training program (transferable to other libraries)
- A public-facing resource guide for parents and teachers

### Budget Indicative Range

$30,000–$50,000 (fit-out, software licensing, facilitator training, documentation)

## Pilot 03 — The Frankston QR Art Walk

**Where:** Frankston CBD and foreshore
**Duration:** 6 months development, permanent installation
**Lead:** Blue Snake Studio

### What It Is

A self-guided creative walk through Frankston, using QR codes embedded into or alongside existing public artworks, walls, and sites of community significance.

Each QR code opens a curated audio/visual experience — a story, a song, a history, a creative provocation — designed by a local artist or community member.

### What Makes It Different

No app download required. No account. No tracking. Each QR code links to a lightweight, static web page that loads in any browser and works offline after first load.

The walk grows over time. New QR codes are added as new community stories are gathered.

### Outcomes

- 10–15 QR-linked creative experiences across Frankston
- A documented system for adding new experiences (community-editable)
- A printed walk map (available at Library and Council offices)
- A model for other councils and communities

### Budget Indicative Range

$20,000–$35,000 (content creation, web development, print, installation)

## Cross-Pilot Principles

All three pilots share:

| Principle | Expression |
|---|---|
| Permanent outcomes | Nothing temporary unless explicitly scoped |
| Community ownership | Assets transfer to community or Council at end |
| Full documentation | Every process documented and made freely available |
| No surveillance | No data collected from participants without explicit, informed consent |
| First Nations awareness | Each pilot follows the First Nations Protocol (document 06) |
