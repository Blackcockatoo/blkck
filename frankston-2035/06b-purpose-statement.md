# Purpose Statement
## First Nations Protocol — Frankston 2035

*Standalone. Can be extracted from any version of this document and handed to any audience.*

---

## Why This Protocol Exists

Frankston 2035 is a civic creativity project. It commissions public art, runs programs in schools and libraries, and creates experiences in public space.

All of it takes place on Country.

The Bunurong people of the Kulin Nation are the Traditional Custodians of the land and waters on which this project operates. Their sovereignty has never been ceded. Their cultural presence in this landscape — visible and invisible — is not a project consideration that can be addressed at the end of the planning process. It is the foundation on which the planning process rests.

This protocol exists because that foundation requires a specific kind of attention, and that attention requires a specific kind of process.

---

## The Key Message

**The first step is not content creation. The first step is respectful process.**

Before any artwork is commissioned, any story is told, any QR code is placed on a footpath, any mural is painted — there is a prior question: have we done the work to be here responsibly?

This protocol describes that work. It is a pathway for listening, for establishing relationship, for seeking permission, and for being accountable to the outcomes. It does not describe cultural content. It does not describe cultural engagement that has already occurred. It describes how Blue Snake Studio will approach that engagement before it happens.

There is no shortcut. There is no good creative outcome that justifies a compromised process. The process is part of the outcome.

---

## What This Protocol Is

A set of binding commitments about how Blue Snake Studio conducts itself in relation to First Nations communities in the Frankston area.

It covers:
- What we acknowledge about where we are starting from (outside)
- What claims we will not make
- How we will approach relationship-building
- How we will listen
- How we will seek permission
- What we will do when permission is not granted
- How we will ensure cultural safety in public space
- How we will engage when projects touch Country, story, or site
- How we will be held accountable

---

## What This Protocol Is Not

- A claim of existing relationship with any First Nations community
- A promise that this project will involve First Nations content or collaboration
- Cultural content of any kind
- A substitute for the formal Registered Aboriginal Party (RAP) process under the Aboriginal Heritage Act 2006 (Vic)
- A checklist that, once completed, confers cultural safety
- A statement that can be cited as evidence of partnership

---

## Who This Protocol Applies To

Blue Snake Studio, and every person acting on behalf of Blue Snake Studio, in every context in which this project operates.

It also serves as a reference document for project partners — Council, Library, schools — so they understand how Blue Snake Studio will behave and what they should expect from us.

---

## Current Status

This protocol has been written by Blue Snake Studio as an internal working document.

**It has not yet been reviewed by any First Nations community, advisor, or organisation.**

That review is a prerequisite for any operational use. Nothing in this protocol may be cited, quoted, or acted upon in relation to any First Nations community until an independent cultural safety advisor has reviewed it and their feedback has been incorporated.

We publish this document internally because transparency is part of accountability.
