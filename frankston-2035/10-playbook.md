# The Frankston Playbook
## A Practical Manual for Civic Creativity

*Version 1.0 — Scaffold*

---

### What This Is

Not a proposal. Not a strategy document. Not a celebration.

A manual.

Every chapter answers one question: *how do you actually do this?*

Written for the person who has just joined the project — or who is about to start one exactly like it, somewhere else.

---

### How to Use It

Read the chapter that matches your situation.
Follow the steps.
Adapt what doesn't fit.
Document what you change.
Share it back.

---

## Chapter Structure

Each chapter follows the same format:

```
1. What this chapter is for
2. Who should read it
3. The core idea in one sentence
4. The process (step by step)
5. What you'll need
6. What can go wrong (and what to do)
7. A real example (added as projects complete)
8. Resources
```

## A Note on Writing This Playbook

The chapters marked *"to be written after X"* are intentional.

A playbook that is written entirely in advance — before the pilots have run, before the relationships have been tested, before anything has gone wrong — is fiction, not instruction.

The best chapters will be written by the people who did the work, about the work they actually did.

That is the only playbook worth reading.
