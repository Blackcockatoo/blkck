Mythology · Symbols · Lore

The world behind the music.

Black Wing Crew isn't a band name chosen for effect — it's a mythological body. The crew is the black cockatoo flock, the omen lineage, the group of artist-ancestors and street voices that populate the B$S universe. The music comes from inside the world, not outside it.

Neon Venom as an LP sits at the intersection of street mythology and the digital city. The black cockatoo appears as deity, harbinger, artist and trickster. The snake coils around the dollar sign. The neon city pulses behind everything. The venom is not poison — it's the truth delivered fast.

Every release has a visual identity: a lyric poster, a QR code, a symbol set. The music and the image always arrive together.

  • The black cockatoo The central recurring figure. Deity, artist, ancestor, omen. Never explained directly.
  • The coiled snake The B$S symbol. Encircles the dollar sign. Knowledge, danger and transformation.
  • The neon city The built world. Cyberpunk, broken, still alive. Where the street print lands.
  • The feather The physical talisman. The thing you find. The thing that starts everything.
  • The dollar sign Inside the snake. Inside the studio name. Not about money — about meaning embedded in commercial form.
  • The QR code The street portal. A physical object pointing into the digital world. The trigger for the whole system.
