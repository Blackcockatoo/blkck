The idea

Frankston has creative people. What it lacks is creative infrastructure.

Frankston 2035 builds that infrastructure permanently. Not events. Not consultancies. Things that stay on walls, in libraries, and in the streets long after the project ends.

A mural that teaches.

Co-designed with students at a Frankston primary school. Curriculum-linked. Painted by a professional artist. On the wall for decades. Owned by the school, no ongoing cost.

A library where you make things.

Frankston Library gets a dedicated creative technology space. Drawing, music, writing, animation — all offline, account-free, ad-free. The tools serve the child, not a corporation.

A walk that tells Frankston's story.

Ten to fifteen stops through the CBD and foreshore. QR codes linking to local art, local voices, local history. No app required. No download. The walk belongs to the city.

Infrastructure, not events.

When the funding ends, the mural is still on the wall. When the program finishes, the library space is still open. When the artist moves on, the walk is still there. This is the difference between a project and a system.

Schools and teachers

Working in a Frankston school?

We are looking for one Frankston primary school to be the site of the first Living Mural — a permanent public artwork co-designed with your students, curriculum-linked, and owned by your school forever. Here is exactly what's on offer and what we need from you.

What your school gets

One permanent large-scale mural, owned by the school at no cost. A curriculum resource pack linked to Visual Arts, Mathematics, English and Humanities. A documented co-design process your students were part of. A credited professional artist who spends time in your school.

What it costs the school

Nothing financial. What we need: one nominated teacher (2–4 hours total), two to three planning sessions, one or two classroom co-design sessions facilitated by BSS, and principal sign-off on the installation.

Healthy technology tools

All software used in Frankston 2035 programs is free, offline-capable, account-free, and ad-free. Krita (drawing), Scratch offline (coding), Audacity (audio), LMMS (music). BSS provides installation guidance and facilitator training at no cost to the school.

The curriculum connection

The Living Mural connects to Visual Arts (Levels 3–6), Mathematics Geometry, English, Humanities — Civics and Citizenship, and Cross-curriculum Critical and Creative Thinking. A full resource pack including lesson plans is delivered before the mural is complete.

To express interest: email us your school's name, your role, and one sentence about why this project appeals to you. We respond within five business days.

Healthy Technology Charter

Three questions. Every technology must pass all three.

Every piece of technology used in or near children in Frankston 2035 programs must answer yes to these three questions. Fail any: the technology is rejected.

Does it serve the child, not the corporation?
Does it work without an account?
Does it work offline, or can it?
No advertising on any screen
No student data collected or transmitted
Free to use — no cost to school or family
Open-source or independently audited
Usable without internet access

What Frankston 2035 is not claiming

  • Not a government program — Frankston 2035 is an initiative of Blue Snake Studio, an independent creative practice. Council partnership is sought, not assumed.
  • Not yet confirmed by Council — All elements of this project are in the proposal stage. No Council endorsement, funding, or partnership has been confirmed as of June 2026.
  • Not a First Nations cultural program — Any First Nations engagement follows a documented 9-part protocol, beginning with listening. No cultural content is used without explicit written permission. The protocol is an internal working document pending review by an independent cultural safety advisor.
  • Not a school-approved curriculum resource — Curriculum connections described have not been formally assessed or endorsed by any school, education department or curriculum authority. They are a design framework, not a guarantee.
  • Not a clinical or therapeutic service — Healthy technology tools and creative learning activities are not clinical instruments and do not replace professional educational, health or disability support services.
  • Not a substitute for qualified advice — Budget estimates are benchmarked, not supplier-quoted. All figures include a 15% contingency. Full claims and status codes are in the Proof Register.
